    To make your UnitMaster URL a valid top-level domain, you need to:

    1. Choose and Register a Domain Name

    Recommended domain options:

    • unitmaster.com (if available)
    • unitmaster.io
    • unitmaster.app
    • myunitmaster.com
    • convertunits.com
    • unitconverter.pro

    Where to register:

    • Namecheap (recommended - affordable, good service)
    • GoDaddy
    • Google Domains (now part of Squarespace)
    • Cloudflare Registrar (at-cost pricing)

    2. Deploy Your App to a Hosting Platform

    Since you're using base44, you'll need to:

    1. Export your app from base44 platform
    2. Choose a hosting service:
      • Vercel (recommended for React apps)
      • Netlify
      • AWS Amplify
      • GitHub Pages

    3. Connect Your Domain

    For Vercel (Recommended):

    1. Deploy your app to Vercel
    2. Go to your project dashboard
    3. Click "Settings" → "Domains"
    4. Add your custom domain
    5. Update DNS records at your domain registrar

    DNS Configuration:

    Type: CNAME
Name: www
Value: your-app.vercel.app

Type: A
Name: @
Value: 76.76.19.61 (Vercel's IP)

    4. Update Your AdSense Configuration

    Once you have your domain:

    1. Apply for Google AdSense with your new domain
    2. Update the AdSense Publisher ID in your Layout.js:
    // Replace this in Layout.js
const ADSENSE_PUBLISHER_ID = "ca-pub-YOUR-ACTUAL-PUBLISHER-ID";
    1. Add your domain to AdSense in your Google AdSense account

    5. SSL Certificate

    Most hosting platforms (Vercel, Netlify) provide free SSL certificates automatically, ensuring your site uses HTTPS.

    Quick Steps Summary:

    1. Register domain (e.g., unitmaster.com)
    2. Deploy to Vercel/Netlify
    3. Connect domain in hosting dashboard
    4. Update DNS at your registrar
    5. Apply for AdSense with new domain
    6. Update AdSense IDs in your code

    Estimated costs:

    • Domain: $10-15/year
    • Hosting: Free (Vercel/Netlify free tiers)
    • SSL: Free (included)
